About Europe

Europe is a continent located entirely in the Northern Hemisphere and mostly in the Eastern Hemisphere. It is bordered by the Arctic Ocean to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the west, and the Mediterranean Sea to the south.


Countries in Europe

Western and Central Europe

France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, Switzerland, Austria, Denmark, Norway, Sweden

Southern Europe

Spain, Italy, Portugal, Greece, Malta, Andorra, San Marino, Vatican City, Monaco, and Cyprus

Northern Europe and Scandinavia

Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, and the United Kingdom

Eastern Europe and The Balkans

Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, Albania, North Macedonia, Kosovo, Slovenia, Ukraine, Belarus, Moldova, Russia, and Turkey

Transcontinental Countries

Russia, Turkiye, Kazakhstan, Azerbaijan, and Georgia


Best Places to Visit in South America

The Colosseum (Rome, Italy)

This is a massive, ancident amphitheater made in 80 AD. It represents an iconic symbol of Roman engineering, hosting dramatic plays and spectacles.

The Eiffel Tower (Paris, France)

This is an 1000 foot iron tower built for the 1889 World's Fair. It was criticized by artists, but later on became a global cultural icon and one of the world's most visited monuments.

La Sagrada Familia (Barcelona, Spain)

This is a breathtaking, unfinished basilica made by Antoni Gaudi. It combines Gothi and Art Nouveau to make organic towers and the Parthenon temple.

The Acropolis (Athens, Greece)

This is an ancident citadel on a rocky outcrop above Athens. It has several historical significant buildings such as the Parthenon temple, most notably.

The Louvre Museum (Paris, France)

This is the world's largest art museum inside a historical royal palace. It has many masterpieces from the years, like Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa.

Buckingham Palace (London, United Kingdom)

This is the official London residence and headquarters of the British monarch. It's known for having grand rooms and the Changing of the Guard ceremony.

Neuschwanstein Castle (Bavaria, Germany)

This is a Romanesque Revival palace nestled in the Alps. It is commissioned by King Ludwig II, but its fairytale design was inspired by the Sleeping Beauty Castle from Disney.
